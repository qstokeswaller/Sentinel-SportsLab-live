## 1. MUSCULOSKELETAL / MOVEMENT SCREENING

### 1.1 FUNCTIONAL MOVEMENT SCREEN (FMS)

**Purpose:** Identify asymmetries and movement dysfunction  
**Equipment:** FMS kit (hurdle, dowel, 2×4 board), measuring tape  
**Scoring:** 0-3 per test (0-21 total)  
**Time:** 10-15 minutes

#### **Test 1: Deep Squat**

**Setup:**
- Athlete holds dowel overhead with arms fully extended
- Feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing forward
- Dowel aligned with feet

**Procedure:**
1. Athlete descends into full squat position
2. Keep heels and dowel aligned over feet
3. Keep torso upright and parallel with tibia
4. Knees aligned over feet
5. Perform 3 repetitions

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Upper torso parallel with tibia, femur below horizontal, knees over feet, dowel aligned over feet
- **2:** Same as 3 but with board under heels (heel lift compensation)
- **1:** Tibia and torso not parallel, femur not below horizontal
- **0:** Pain anywhere during test

---

#### **Test 2: Hurdle Step**

**Setup:**
- Hurdle height set to tibial tuberosity height
- Dowel held across shoulders
- Stand with toes touching base of hurdle

**Procedure:**
1. Lift one leg and step over hurdle
2. Touch heel to floor on other side
3. Return to starting position maintaining balance
4. Repeat 3 times each leg

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Hips, knees, ankles aligned, no torso movement, dowel stays level
- **2:** Movement completed with compensations (loss of alignment)
- **1:** Loss of balance, contact with hurdle
- **0:** Pain during test

**Record:** Lowest score of left/right

---

#### **Test 3: In-Line Lunge**

**Setup:**
- Athlete's tibial length measured
- Dowel held behind back (touching head, thoracic spine, sacrum)
- Stance foot on line, opposite foot behind at tibial length distance

**Procedure:**
1. Descend into lunge position
2. Touch back knee to line behind front heel
3. Return to start
4. Perform 3 repetitions each side

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Dowel contacts maintained, knee touches line, no torso movement
- **2:** Dowel contacts maintained but torso movement, heel rises, or knee doesn't touch
- **1:** Loss of balance, dowel loses contact
- **0:** Pain during test

**Record:** Lowest score of left/right

---

#### **Test 4: Shoulder Mobility**

**Setup:**
- Measure hand length from distal wrist crease to tip of longest finger
- Make fists with thumbs inside

**Procedure:**
1. Athlete simultaneously reaches one hand over shoulder (down back) and one hand up back
2. Note which hand is on top
3. Measure distance between closest bony prominences (fists)
4. Repeat opposite direction
5. Perform clearing test (impingement)

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Fists within one hand-length
- **2:** Fists within 1.5 hand-lengths
- **1:** Fists not within 1.5 hand-lengths
- **0:** Pain during test or clearing

**Record:** Lowest score of left/right

---

#### **Test 5: Active Straight Leg Raise**

**Setup:**
- Athlete supine
- Dowel placed at ASIS level across thighs
- Arms at sides, palms up
- Opposite leg remains flat

**Procedure:**
1. Lift one leg with knee extended, ankle dorsiflexed
2. Raise as high as possible
3. Opposite leg, head, and arms stay on ground
4. Repeat 3 times each leg

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Malleolus between mid-thigh and ASIS
- **2:** Malleolus between mid-thigh and joint line
- **1:** Malleolus below joint line
- **0:** Pain during test

**Record:** Lowest score of left/right

---

#### **Test 6: Trunk Stability Push-Up**

**Setup:**
- Prone position
- Men: hands at forehead level
- Women: hands at chin level
- Feet together

**Procedure:**
1. Hands placed shoulder-width apart
2. Perform one push-up maintaining body alignment
3. If unable, move hands to lower position (chin for men, clavicle for women)
4. Perform one rep

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Males push from forehead, females from chin position, body lifts as unit
- **2:** Males push from chin, females from clavicle, body lifts as unit
- **1:** Unable to perform one repetition in alignment
- **0:** Pain during test

---

#### **Test 7: Rotary Stability**

**Setup:**
- Quadruped position on board
- Hands under shoulders, knees under hips

**Procedure:**
1. Simultaneously lift same-side hand and knee 6 inches
2. Reach hand forward and knee backward
3. Touch elbow to knee
4. Return hand and knee to starting position
5. If unable, perform diagonal pattern (opposite hand/knee)
6. Test both sides

**Scoring:**
- **3:** Same-side pattern completed, maintains board contact
- **2:** Diagonal pattern completed, maintains contact
- **1:** Unable to complete diagonal pattern
- **0:** Pain during test

**Record:** Lowest score of left/right

---

**FMS COMPOSITE SCORING:**
- **Total Score:** Sum of 7 tests (0-21)
- **Interpretation:**
  - ≥14: Low injury risk
  - ≤14: Increased injury risk (2-4x)
  - Asymmetry (difference between L/R): Increased risk
  - Pain (score of 0): Medical referral

### 1.2 Y-BALANCE TEST (YBT)

**Purpose:** Assess dynamic balance and asymmetry  
**Equipment:** Y-Balance Kit or tape measure  
**Time:** 10-15 minutes  
**Population:** All athletes, especially those with ankle/knee/hip injury risk

**Setup:**
- Three lines marked at 120° angles: Anterior, Posteromedial, Posterolateral
- Athlete stands barefoot on test leg at center
- Hands on hips

**Procedure:**
1. **Familiarization:** 6 practice trials per direction per leg
2. **Testing:** 3 trials per direction per leg
3. Push reach indicator as far as possible with opposite leg
4. Maintain single-leg stance throughout
5. Return to bilateral stance with control
6. Rest 15 seconds between trials

**Invalid Trial (repeat):**
- Hands leave hips
- Stance foot lifts or shifts
- Fail to return to start
- Touch down with reach foot for support

**Measurements:**
- Record maximum reach distance (cm) for each direction
- Take best of 3 trials per direction
- Measure both legs

**Calculations:**
- **Anterior Reach (%)** = (Anterior Distance / Leg Length) × 100
- **Posteromedial Reach (%)** = (PM Distance / Leg Length) × 100
- **Posterolateral Reach (%)** = (PL Distance / Leg Length) × 100
- **Composite Score** = (ANT + PM + PL) / (Leg Length × 3) × 100

**Leg Length:** ASIS to medial malleolus

**Normative Data:**
- Composite Score >94% = Good
- Composite Score <89% = Increased injury risk
- Anterior reach asymmetry >4cm = 2.5x injury risk

**Interpretation:**
- Asymmetry >4cm: Increased risk (2.5x)
- Low composite scores: Decreased neuromuscular control
- Anterior direction most predictive of lower extremity injury

### 1.3 SINGLE LEG BALANCE TEST

**Purpose:** Assess static balance and proprioception  
**Equipment:** Stopwatch, firm surface  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Positions:** Eyes open, Eyes closed

**Protocol - Eyes Open:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Hands on hips
3. Opposite knee bent to 90°
4. Maintain position for up to 60 seconds
5. Test both legs

**Protocol - Eyes Closed:**
1. Same setup as eyes open
2. Close eyes once balanced
3. Maintain for up to 45 seconds

**Termination Criteria:**
- Hands leave hips
- Stance foot shifts/hops
- Opposite foot touches ground
- Eyes open (if eyes-closed test)
- 60 seconds elapsed (eyes open) or 45 seconds (eyes closed)

**Scoring:**
- Record time (seconds) until termination
- Perform 3 trials, record best
- Test both legs

**Normative Data (Eyes Open):**
- Excellent: >50 seconds
- Good: 40-50 seconds
- Average: 25-40 seconds
- Below Average: <25 seconds

**Normative Data (Eyes Closed):**
- Excellent: >30 seconds
- Good: 20-30 seconds
- Average: 10-20 seconds
- Below Average: <10 seconds

**Asymmetry:** >10% difference between legs may indicate deficit

### 1.4 NORDBORD HAMSTRING STRENGTH TEST

**Purpose:** Assess eccentric hamstring strength and injury risk  
**Equipment:** NordBord device (VALD Performance) or manual load cell  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Football/soccer, rugby, track & field

**Setup:**
1. Athlete kneels on padded platform
2. Ankles secured under ankle hooks/pads
3. Hooks positioned at top of shoes, perpendicular to leg
4. Body starts in upright position, neutral hips
5. Arms crossed over chest or at sides

**Familiarization:**
- **CRITICAL:** Minimum 2 familiarization sessions required
- Practice Nordic hamstring curls 3-5 days before testing
- 5-10 sub-maximal reps per session

**Testing Protocol:**
1. Athlete assumes kneeling position
2. Maintain straight torso alignment (hips to shoulders in line)
3. Lower body forward as slowly as possible
4. Resist forward motion using hamstrings (eccentric contraction)
5. Continue lowering, increasing knee angle
6. Control descent until unable to resist
7. Catch self with hands when control lost
8. Perform **3 maximal repetitions**
9. Rest **30-60 seconds** between reps
10. Record average peak force from 3 trials

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Force (Newtons)** - highest force during eccentric phase
- **Relative Peak Force (N/kg bodyweight)**
- **Left vs Right Asymmetry (%)**
- **Force-Time Curve** - rate of force development

**Normative Data:**
- **Injury Risk Threshold:** <337-340N = 4x injury risk
- **Safe Zone:** >340N both limbs
- **Elite Athletes:** 400-550N
- **Sub-Elite:** 300-400N
- **Recreational:** 250-350N

**Asymmetry Thresholds:**
- <10%: Excellent symmetry
- 10-15%: Acceptable
- >15%: High risk - address imbalance

**Normative by Age (Youth):**
- U12: 150N ± 15N
- U14: 200N ± 20N
- U16: 280N ± 30N
- U18: 330N ± 40N
- Adult: 400N ± 60N

**Relative Strength (N/kg):**
- Elite: >5.5 N/kg
- Good: 4.5-5.5 N/kg
- Average: 3.5-4.5 N/kg
- Below Average: <3.5 N/kg

**Alternative Protocol (without NordBord):**
- Partner-resisted Nordic curls with manual load cell
- Measured force at ankles
- Less standardized but still informative

**Interpretation:**
- Low force (<340N): Needs eccentric hamstring strengthening
- High asymmetry (>15%): Unilateral deficit, injury risk
- Combine with: Sprint testing, IMTP for full lower body profile

**Equipment:**
- **NordBord (VALD):** ~$4,000-5,000 (subscription model)
- **DIY Load Cell Setup:** ~$500-1,000

### 1.5 HIP ADDUCTION SQUEEZE TEST - SHORT LEVER (GroinBar/ForceFrame)

**Purpose:** Assess bilateral hip adductor strength and groin injury risk  
**Equipment:** GroinBar or ForceFrame (VALD Performance)  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Football/soccer, ice hockey, rugby, Gaelic football

**Setup:**
1. Athlete lies supine on floor/mat
2. **Standard position: 60° knee flexion** (can test at 0°, 45°, 90°)
3. Hips neutral (0° flexion for 60° knee position)
4. Place inner knees on load cells of GroinBar/ForceFrame
5. Feet flat on ground, shoulder-width apart
6. Arms crossed on chest or at sides

**Testing Procedure:**
1. Athlete positions knees against inner sensors/pads
2. Command: "Squeeze your knees together as hard as you can"
3. **5-second maximal isometric contraction**
4. Verbal encouragement: "Go ahead - push - push - push - push - and relax"
5. Rest **30 seconds**
6. Perform **3 maximal trials**
7. Record best trial from each leg

**Alternative Testing Positions:**
- **0° hip flexion:** Legs flat (lower force output)
- **45° hip flexion:** Optimal position - highest adductor activity
- **60° hip flexion:** Standard ForceFrame position
- **90° hip flexion:** Hip and knee both at 90°

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Force (Newtons)** - each leg independently
- **Relative Strength (N/kg bodyweight)**
- **Left-Right Asymmetry (%)**
- **Add:Abd Ratio** (when combined with abduction test)
- **Pain Provocation (0-10 NRS scale)**

**Normative Values (60° knee flexion):**

**Males:**
- Elite: 3.5-4.5 N/kg
- Good: 2.8-3.5 N/kg
- Average: 2.0-2.8 N/kg
- Below Average: <2.0 N/kg

**Females:**
- Elite: 3.0-4.0 N/kg
- Good: 2.5-3.0 N/kg
- Average: 1.8-2.5 N/kg
- Below Average: <1.8 N/kg

**Adduction:Abduction Ratio:**
- **Ideal:** 0.90-1.10
- **Amber Zone:** 0.80-0.90
- **Red Flag:** <0.80 (4-5x injury risk)

**Asymmetry Thresholds:**
- <5%: Excellent
- 5-10%: Acceptable
- 10-15%: Monitor
- >15%: High risk

**Injury Risk Indicators:**
- Absolute weakness: <2.5 N/kg
- Low Add:Abd ratio: <0.80
- High asymmetry: >15%
- Pain on test: NRS >2/10 = subclinical injury

**Pain Provocation:**
- **0-1:** No concern
- **2-4:** Monitor, possible subclinical injury
- **5+:** Medical evaluation required

**Equipment Cost:**
- **GroinBar (VALD):** ~$3,500-4,500
- **ForceFrame (VALD):** ~$3,000-4,000

**Note:** This is the test where you squeeze your groins/knees INWARD onto the machine

### 1.6 HIP ABDUCTION PRESS TEST - SHORT LEVER (GroinBar/ForceFrame)

**Purpose:** Assess bilateral hip abductor strength (gluteus medius/minimus)  
**Equipment:** GroinBar or ForceFrame (VALD Performance)  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Football/soccer, ice hockey, rugby

**Setup:**
1. Athlete lies supine on floor/mat
2. **Standard position: 60° knee flexion**
3. Hips neutral
4. Place **outer knees/thighs** on outer load cells
5. Feet together or slightly apart
6. Arms crossed on chest

**Testing Procedure:**
1. Athlete positions outer knees against outer sensors
2. Command: "Push your knees outward as hard as you can"
3. **5-second maximal isometric contraction**
4. Prevent feet from sliding apart (use wall or strap if needed)
5. Rest **30 seconds**
6. Perform **3 maximal trials**
7. Record best trial from each leg

**Alternative Positions:**
- **0° hip flexion:** Legs extended
- **45° hip flexion**
- **60° hip flexion:** Standard
- **90° hip flexion**

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Force (Newtons)** - each leg
- **Relative Strength (N/kg bodyweight)**
- **Left-Right Asymmetry (%)**
- **Add:Abd Ratio**
- **Rate of Force Development (RFD 0-100ms, 0-200ms)** if device capable

**Normative Values (60° knee flexion):**

**Males:**
- Elite: 3.0-4.0 N/kg
- Good: 2.5-3.0 N/kg
- Average: 2.0-2.5 N/kg
- Below Average: <2.0 N/kg

**Females:**
- Elite: 2.5-3.5 N/kg
- Good: 2.0-2.5 N/kg
- Average: 1.5-2.0 N/kg
- Below Average: <1.5 N/kg

**Add:Abd Ratio Calculation:**
- Ratio = Adduction Strength / Abduction Strength
- **Target:** 0.90-1.10
- <0.80 indicates adductor weakness (groin injury risk)
- >1.20 indicates abductor weakness (pelvic stability concern)

**Muscles Tested:**
- Gluteus medius (primary)
- Gluteus minimus (primary)
- Tensor fasciae latae (TFL)

**Functional Importance:**
- Pelvic stability during single-leg stance
- Lateral stability during cutting/COD
- Critical for skating (ice hockey)
- Hip/knee alignment during landing

**Equipment:** Same device as adduction test

**Note:** This is the test where you push your knees OUTWARD against the machine (tests glutes)

### 1.7 HIP ADDUCTION SQUEEZE TEST - LONG LEVER (Copenhagen 5-Second Squeeze)

**Purpose:** Hip adductor strength with straight legs - more sensitive to groin pain  
**Equipment:** Handheld dynamometer (HHD) with external fixation OR ForceFrame  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Football/soccer, ice hockey

**Setup:**
1. Athlete supine on treatment table/mat
2. **Hips and knees fully extended** (straight legs)
3. Legs **slightly abducted** (~20-30cm apart)
4. Arms at sides or hands on chest
5. Resistance placed **5cm above medial malleolus** (ankle)

**Testing Procedure:**
1. Examiner places HHD between ankles (or athlete's ankles on ForceFrame sensors)
2. Examiner's forearm/HHD width determines leg spacing
3. Command: "Squeeze your legs together as hard as you can for 5 seconds"
4. Athlete performs bilateral hip adduction
5. Record peak torque (Nm/kg)
6. Rest **30 seconds**
7. Perform **2-3 trials**
8. Record any groin pain (0-10 NRS)

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Torque (Nm/kg bodyweight)**
- **Asymmetry** (if measured unilaterally)
- **Pain Provocation (NRS 0-10)**

**Normative Values:**

**Males:**
- Elite: 3.5-4.5 Nm/kg
- Good: 3.0-3.5 Nm/kg
- Average: 2.5-3.0 Nm/kg
- Below Average: <2.5 Nm/kg

**Females:**
- Elite: 3.0-4.0 Nm/kg
- Good: 2.5-3.0 Nm/kg
- Average: 2.0-2.5 Nm/kg
- Below Average: <2.0 Nm/kg

**Comparison to Short-Lever:**
- Long-lever produces **~15-20% higher torque** than ForceFrame short-lever
- **More sensitive** to groin pain detection
- Better reflects functional adductor capacity

**Pain Interpretation:**
- NRS >2: Subclinical groin injury concern
- Pain more common in long-lever vs short-lever

**Equipment:**
- Handheld Dynamometer: $500-2,000
- Requires external fixation (belt/wall) for reliability

**Advantage:** Better pain provocation, higher torque output  
**Disadvantage:** Requires more setup/fixation than GroinBar

### 1.8 HIP ABDUCTION PRESS TEST - LONG LEVER

**Purpose:** Hip abductor strength with straight legs  
**Equipment:** HHD with belt fixation or modified ForceFrame  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Research/clinical settings

**Setup:**
1. Supine, hips and knees extended
2. Resistance at lateral ankles (5cm above lateral malleolus)
3. Belt or rigid fixation for HHD
4. Legs together at start

**Testing Procedure:**
1. Push legs apart against resistance
2. 5-second maximal contraction
3. 2-3 trials
4. Record peak force

**Measurements:**
- Peak Force (N)
- Peak Torque (Nm/kg)
- RFD (if capable)

**Note:** Less commonly used than short-lever; novel test with limited normative data

### 1.9 HIP INTERNAL ROTATION TEST (Prone)

**Purpose:** Assess hip IR range of motion  
**Equipment:** Goniometer or inclinometer  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Population:** Hip/groin screening, FAI assessment

**Setup:**
1. Athlete prone on table
2. Knee bent to 90°
3. Hip neutral (no rotation)
4. Pelvis stabilized

**Procedure:**
1. Examiner passively rotates lower leg **outward** (= hip internal rotation)
2. Stop at end ROM (firm resistance or compensatory pelvic movement)
3. Measure angle with goniometer
4. Test both hips

**Measurement:**
- Angle from vertical (0°) to lower leg position
- Record degrees of IR

**Normative ROM:**
- Normal: 35-45°
- Limited: <30°
- Excessive: >50° (hypermobility concern)

**Asymmetry:**
- >10° difference = potential concern
- <5° difference = normal variation

**Clinical Significance:**
- Limited IR: Associated with hip impingement, labral tears
- Asymmetric IR: May contribute to compensatory movement patterns
- Combined with FABER test for comprehensive hip assessment

### 1.10 HIP EXTERNAL ROTATION TEST

**Purpose:** Assess hip ER range of motion  
**Equipment:** Goniometer  
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Prone, knee bent 90°
2. Hip neutral

**Procedure:**
1. Passively rotate lower leg **inward** (= hip external rotation)
2. Measure angle
3. Test both sides

**Normative ROM:**
- Normal: 40-50°
- Limited: <35°

**Asymmetry:** >10° difference may indicate restriction

### 1.11 OVERHEAD SQUAT ASSESSMENT

**Purpose:** Screen for mobility limitations and movement patterns  
**Equipment:** Dowel or PVC pipe  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Population:** General fitness, movement screening

**Setup:**
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. Toes pointing forward or slightly outward
3. Hold dowel overhead, arms fully extended
4. Hands 1.5x shoulder-width grip

**Procedure:**
1. Athlete descends into full squat
2. Assess from anterior, lateral, and posterior views
3. Perform 5 repetitions
4. Observe movement quality and compensations

**Assessment Points:**

**From Front View:**
- **Feet:** Turn out? Flatten?
- **Knees:** Cave inward (valgus)? Bow outward (varus)?
- **Symmetry:** Left vs right differences?

**From Side View:**
- **Arms:** Fall forward? Stay overhead?
- **Torso:** Excessive forward lean?
- **Heels:** Lift off ground?
- **Hip Depth:** Break parallel?

**Scoring:**
- **Pass:** Can perform with good alignment, minimal compensation
- **Fail:** Compensation patterns present

**Common Compensations & Likely Causes:**

| Compensation | Likely Limitation |
|---|---|
| Arms fall forward | Shoulder/thoracic mobility |
| Heels lift | Ankle dorsiflexion restriction |
| Knees cave inward | Hip abductor/glute weakness, adductor tightness |
| Excessive forward torso | Hip mobility OR ankle restriction |
| Low back arches | Hip flexor tightness, core weakness |

**Modifications:**
- **Heels elevated:** Differentiates ankle vs hip restriction
- **Hands on hips:** Removes shoulder component

**Use:** 
- Pre-participation screening
- Return-to-sport assessment
- Movement pattern identification

### 1.12 THOMAS TEST (Hip Flexor Length)

**Purpose:** Assess hip flexor flexibility (iliopsoas, rectus femoris)  
**Equipment:** Treatment table  
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Athlete sits at edge of table
2. Lies back, pulling one knee to chest
3. Opposite leg hangs off edge

**Procedure:**
1. Athlete holds one knee to chest (flattens lumbar spine)
2. Observe position of opposite (test) leg
3. Note: thigh position, knee angle, hip rotation
4. Test both sides

**Interpretation:**

**Normal:**
- Thigh remains on table (or slightly below horizontal)
- Knee flexed 80-90°
- No compensatory movements

**Restrictions:**

| Finding | Indication |
|---|---|
| Thigh lifts off table | Hip flexor (iliopsoas) tightness |
| Knee extends >90° | Rectus femoris tightness |
| Thigh abducts | TFL/IT band tightness |
| Hip externally rotates | Piriformis involvement |

**Grading:**
- **0:** Thigh below horizontal = excellent
- **1:** Thigh horizontal = normal
- **2:** Thigh elevated 15-30° = moderate tightness
- **3:** Thigh elevated >30° = severe tightness

### 1.13 ANKLE DORSIFLEXION TEST (Weight-Bearing Lunge)

**Purpose:** Assess ankle DF ROM - critical for squat, jump, landing  
**Equipment:** Tape measure or ruler  
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Facing wall
2. Test foot at measured distance from wall
3. Opposite leg back in lunge stance

**Procedure:**
1. Start with toe 10cm from wall
2. Lunge forward, attempting to touch knee to wall
3. Keep heel down, no arch collapse
4. If successful, move toe back 1cm, repeat
5. Continue until knee can no longer touch wall
6. Record maximum distance
7. Test both ankles

**Normative Data:**
- Excellent: >12cm
- Good: 10-12cm
- Average: 8-10cm
- Limited: 6-8cm
- Severe Restriction: <6cm

**Functional Requirements:**
- Squatting: 10-12cm minimum
- Running mechanics: 8-10cm
- ACL injury risk: <8cm associated with increased risk

**Asymmetry:** >1.5cm difference = potential concern

### 1.14 SINGLE LEG SQUAT ASSESSMENT

**Purpose:** Assess dynamic lower extremity control and identify compensations  
**Equipment:** None (mirror helpful)  
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Arms across chest or out front for balance
3. Opposite leg off ground

**Procedure:**
1. Squat down to 60° knee flexion (thigh roughly parallel to ground)
2. Return to start
3. Perform 5 repetitions
4. Assess from front and side views
5. Test both legs

**Assessment Criteria:**

**Frontal Plane (Front View):**
- Knee alignment over 2nd toe (NO valgus collapse)
- Trunk remains upright (minimal lateral lean)
- Pelvis stays level (no Trendelenburg)
- Arms symmetric

**Sagittal Plane (Side View):**
- Controlled descent/ascent
- Knee travels forward appropriately
- Minimal forward trunk lean
- Maintains balance

**Scoring:**
- **Good:** Controlled, aligned, 5 reps without compensation
- **Fair:** Minor compensations, completes 5 reps
- **Poor:** Major compensations, unable to complete 5 reps

**Common Compensations:**

| Compensation | Likely Cause |
|---|---|
| Knee valgus (cave in) | Glute weakness, hip ER weakness |
| Trunk lean toward stance leg | Glute medius weakness |
| Trunk lean away from stance leg | Glute max weakness |
| Arms flailing | Balance/proprioception deficit |
| Inability to reach depth | Strength or mobility limitation |

### 1.15 TRUNK STABILITY TESTS

**Purpose:** Assess core endurance and stability  
**Equipment:** Stopwatch, mat  
**Time:** 15 minutes

#### **Front Plank (Prone Hold)**

**Setup:**
- Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders
- Legs extended, toes on ground
- Body in straight line

**Procedure:**
1. Hold position, maintaining alignment
2. Time to failure
3. Terminate when: hips sag, hips pike, cannot maintain position

**Normative (seconds):**
- Excellent: >90 seconds
- Good: 60-90 seconds
- Average: 30-60 seconds
- Below: <30 seconds

---

#### **Side Plank**

**Setup:**
- Side-lying, forearm on ground
- Elbow under shoulder
- Feet stacked or staggered
- Hips lifted, straight line

**Procedure:**
1. Hold position
2. Time to failure
3. Test both sides

**Normative (seconds):**
- Excellent: >75 seconds
- Good: 50-75 seconds
- Average: 25-50 seconds
- Below: <25 seconds

**Asymmetry:** >10 second difference = imbalance

## 2. STRENGTH / POWER

### 2.1 ONE-REPETITION MAXIMUM (1RM) TESTING

**Purpose:** Assess maximal strength  
**Equipment:** Barbell, plates, rack/platform  
**Time:** 30-45 minutes per lift  
**Safety:** Requires spotters, proper technique

**General 1RM Protocol:**

**Warm-Up:**
1. **Set 1:** 5-10 reps @ 40-50% estimated 1RM
2. **Set 2:** 3-5 reps @ 60-70% estimated 1RM
3. **Set 3:** 2-3 reps @ 75-85% estimated 1RM
4. **Set 4:** 1 rep @ 90% estimated 1RM
5. Rest 3-5 minutes

**1RM Attempts:**
1. Attempt estimated 1RM
2. If successful, rest 3-5 minutes, increase 2.5-5% (lower body) or 2.5% (upper body)
3. If failed, rest 3-5 minutes, decrease 2.5-5%
4. Maximum 3-5 attempts to find true 1RM
5. Record highest successful lift

**Safety Considerations:**
- Experienced lifters only
- Proper technique mandatory
- Qualified spotters required
- Stop if technique breaks down
- Medical clearance for individuals with health concerns

### 2.2 1RM BACK SQUAT

**Purpose:** Lower body maximal strength  
**Population:** Strength-trained athletes

**Setup:**
- Barbell at shoulder height in rack
- Athlete under bar, bar on upper traps (high-bar) or posterior delts (low-bar)

- Feet shoulder-width to slightly wider

**Procedure:**
1. Unrack bar, step back
2. Feet set, toes slightly out
3. Descend with control until:
   - Hip crease below top of knee (parallel+), OR
   - Sport-specific depth
4. Drive up explosively
5. Return to full extension

**Success Criteria:**
- Adequate depth achieved
- No knee valgus
- Controlled descent
- Complete lockout

**Normative Data (kg relative to bodyweight):**

**Males:**

**Females:**

- Novice: 0.5-0.75x BW

### 2.5 ISOMETRIC MID-THIGH PULL (IMTP) - BASIC

**Purpose:** Assess maximal isometric strength and rate of force development  
**Equipment:** Force plate, IMTP rig with immovable bar  
**Time:** 15 minutes  
**Population:** All strength/power athletes

**Setup:**
1. Athlete stands on force plate
2. Bar height adjusted to mid-thigh position
3. **Knee angle:** 125-145° (most common: 140°)
4. **Hip angle:** 140-150°
5. **Grip:** Shoulder-width, mixed or double overhand
6. Upright torso, neutral spine
7. Arms straight

**Procedure:**
1. Assume position, create tension
2. "Pull as hard and fast as possible" on command
3. **3-5 second maximal effort**
4. Verbal encouragement throughout
5. Rest **2-3 minutes**
6. Perform **2-3 trials**
7. Record best trial

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Force (Newtons)**
- **Relative Peak Force (N/kg)**
- **Time to Peak Force (seconds)**

**Normative Data (N/kg):**

**Males:**
- Elite: >40 N/kg
- Good: 35-40 N/kg
- Average: 30-35 N/kg
- Below: <30 N/kg

**Females:**
- Elite: >35 N/kg
- Good: 30-35 N/kg
- Average: 25-30 N/kg
- Below: <25 N/kg

**Reliability:** ICC >0.90 when standardized

### 2.7 COUNTERMOVEMENT JUMP (CMJ) - BASIC

**Purpose:** Assess lower body power  
**Equipment:** Jump mat, contact mat, force plate, or Vertec  
**Time:** 10 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Stand on mat/plate
2. Hands on hips OR arm swing allowed (specify protocol)
3. Feet shoulder-width apart

**Procedure:**
1. From standing, quickly dip down (countermovement)
2. Immediately jump as high as possible
3. Land on same spot
4. Perform **3-5 trials**
5. Rest **30-60 seconds** between jumps
6. Record best trial

**Measurement:**
- **Jump Height (cm)** - calculated from flight time or force plate data
- **Flight Time (seconds)**

**Normative Data (cm) - Hands on Hips:**

**Males:**
- Elite: >55cm
- Good: 45-55cm
- Average: 35-45cm
- Below: <35cm

**Females:**
- Elite: >45cm
- Good: 35-45cm
- Average: 25-35cm
- Below: <25cm

**With Arm Swing:** Add ~10cm to above values

**Calculation (Flight Time Method):**
- Jump Height (m) = (g × t²) / 8
- g = 9.81 m/s²
- t = flight time (seconds)

### 2.9 SQUAT JUMP (SJ)

**Purpose:** Assess concentric-only power (no stretch-shortening cycle)  
**Equipment:** Jump mat or force plate  
**Time:** 10 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Stand on mat/plate
2. Hands on hips
3. Feet shoulder-width

**Procedure:**
1. Descend to 90° knee angle
2. **Pause for 2-3 seconds** (eliminate stretch reflex)
3. Jump as high as possible from static position
4. No countermovement allowed
5. Land on same spot
6. 3-5 trials
7. Rest 60 seconds between jumps

**Invalid Trial:**
- Countermovement before jump
- Hands leave hips
- Insufficient pause in bottom position

**Measurements:**
- Jump Height (cm)
- Peak Power (W/kg)

**Normative (cm):**

**Males:**
- Elite: >45cm
- Good: 35-45cm
- Average: 25-35cm

**Females:**
- Elite: >35cm
- Good: 25-35cm
- Average: 18-25cm

**SJ vs CMJ Ratio:**
- **CMJ > SJ by 10-20%:** Normal (elastic energy contribution)
- **CMJ < SJ:** Poor stretch-shortening cycle efficiency
- **CMJ >> SJ (>25% difference):** Excellent elastic properties

### 2.10 DROP JUMP TEST

**Purpose:** Assess reactive strength, stretch-shortening cycle  
**Equipment:** Force plate or jump mat, boxes (15-60cm)  
**Time:** 15-20 minutes

**Setup:**
1. Athlete stands on box edge
2. Force plate/mat directly in front
3. Hands on hips throughout
4. Feet shoulder-width

**Procedure:**
1. **Step off** box (do not jump)
2. Land on both feet simultaneously
3. **Immediately** jump vertically as high as possible
4. Minimize ground contact time
5. Land back on mat/plate
6. Perform 2-3 trials per height
7. Rest 30-60 seconds between trials

**Heights Tested:**
- 15cm, 30cm, 45cm, 60cm (progressive)

**Critical Instructions:**
- "Jump as HIGH as possible, as QUICKLY as possible"
- "Spend minimal time on the ground"
- Emphasize both height AND speed

**Measurements:**
- **Jump Height (m or cm)**
- **Ground Contact Time (GCT) (seconds)**
- **Flight Time (seconds)**
- **Reactive Strength Index (RSI)**

**RSI Calculation:**
- **RSI = Jump Height (m) / Ground Contact Time (s)**
- Example: 0.35m jump / 0.25s GCT = RSI of 1.40

**Alternative Calculation:**
- RSI (mm/ms) = Jump Height (mm) / GCT (ms)

**Normative Data - RSI (m/s):**

**Males:**
- Elite: >2.50
- Excellent: 2.00-2.50
- Good: 1.50-2.00
- Average: 1.00-1.50
- Below: <1.00

**Females:**
- Elite: >2.00
- Excellent: 1.50-2.00
- Good: 1.00-1.50
- Average: 0.75-1.00
- Below: <0.75

**Ground Contact Time:**
- Elite: <0.180 seconds
- Good: 0.180-0.250 seconds
- Average: 0.250-0.350 seconds

**Interpretation:**
- **High RSI:** Efficient stretch-shortening cycle, good reactive strength
- **Low RSI:** Poor SSC efficiency, needs plyometric development
- **Optimal Drop Height:** Height where RSI is highest (typically 30-45cm)

---

### 2.11 INCREMENTAL DROP JUMP TEST

**Purpose:** Determine optimal drop height for training prescription  
**Equipment:** Force plate/mat, multiple boxes (15-60cm)  
**Time:** 20 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. Perform drop jumps from 15cm, 30cm, 45cm, 60cm
2. 2-3 trials per height
3. Calculate RSI for each height
4. Plot RSI vs drop height
5. Identify peak RSI = **optimal drop height**

**Interpretation:**

**Normal Profile:**
- RSI increases from 15cm → peaks at 30-45cm → declines at 60cm
- Peak indicates optimal eccentric loading
- Decline indicates exceeded eccentric strength capacity

**Flat Profile:**
- RSI similar across all heights
- May benefit from higher intensities
- Good eccentric strength reserve

**Early Peak (15-30cm):**
- Eccentric strength limitation
- Start plyometrics at lower heights
- Progress gradually

**No Peak (continuous increase):**
- May need to test higher boxes
- Very good eccentric strength

**Use:**
- Individualize plyometric training
- Prescribe appropriate drop heights
- Monitor adaptation over time

---

### 2.12 REACTIVE STRENGTH INDEX (RSI)

**Purpose:** Quantify stretch-shortening cycle efficiency  
**Equipment:** Force plate (gold standard) or jump mat  
**Time:** 15 minutes  
**Population:** ALL explosive sport athletes

**(See Drop Jump Test 2.10 for full protocol)**

**Key Points:**
- Most important plyometric assessment
- Reflects ability to use elastic energy
- Critical for sprint performance
- Predicts COD and agility performance

**Monitoring:**
- Baseline testing: Comprehensive (15-60cm)
- In-season: Single height (optimal) for monitoring
- Frequency: Every 4-6 weeks

**Training Implications:**

| RSI Profile | Training Focus |
|---|---|
| Low RSI, High CMJ | Good strength, poor reactivity → plyometrics |
| High RSI, Low CMJ | Good reactivity, need strength → resistance training |
| Low RSI, Low CMJ | Develop both → foundational strength first |
| High RSI, High CMJ | Well-developed → maintain or progress intensity |

---

### 2.13 MODIFIED REACTIVE STRENGTH INDEX (RSImod)

**Purpose:** Monitor fatigue and reactive strength using CMJ  
**Equipment:** Force plate or jump mat  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Population:** All athletes - ideal for daily monitoring

**Protocol:**
1. Perform standard CMJ (3-5 trials)
2. Measure:
   - **Flight Time** (seconds)
   - **Contraction Time** (time from start of countermovement to takeoff)
3. Calculate RSImod

**RSImod Calculation:**
- **RSImod = Flight Time / Contraction Time**
- OR
- **RSImod = Jump Height / Time to Takeoff**

**Example:**
- Flight Time: 0.55 seconds
- Contraction Time: 0.85 seconds
- RSImod = 0.55 / 0.85 = **0.65**

**Normative Data:**

**General Athletes:**
- Elite: >0.60
- Good: 0.40-0.60
- Average: 0.30-0.40
- Below: <0.30

**Sport-Specific:**
- **Sprinters/Jumpers:** >0.70
- **Team Sports:** >0.50
- **Endurance:** >0.40

**Daily Monitoring:**
- Baseline: Average of 5 non-fatigued days
- Monitor: Daily or post-training
- **Green (>95% baseline):** Recovered
- **Amber (90-95% baseline):** Fatigued
- **Red (<90% baseline):** Highly fatigued

**Advantages:**
- Less fatiguing than drop jumps
- Can be done daily
- Requires minimal equipment
- Sensitive to neuromuscular fatigue

**Use Cases:**
- Daily readiness assessment
- Training load monitoring
- Overtraining detection
- Return-to-play progressions

### 2.15 SINGLE LEG HOP FOR DISTANCE

**Purpose:** Assess unilateral power, return-to-play metric  
**Equipment:** Tape measure

- ACL return-to-play battery

### 2.19 LOADED CMJ (Velocity-Load Profile)

**Purpose:** Assess force-velocity relationship  
**Equipment:** Force plate, trap bar or barbell  
**Time:** 30 minutes

**Setup:**

### 2.20 WINGATE ANAEROBIC TEST

**Purpose:** Assess anaerobic power and capacity  
**Equipment:** Cycle ergometer (Monark or similar)  
**Time:** 30 minutes total

**Setup:**
1. Bike with adjustable resistance
2. Resistance = **7.5% of bodyweight** (standard)
3. Toe clips/straps secured

**Warm-Up:**
1. 5 minutes low-intensity cycling
2. 2-3 short sprints (5 seconds each)
3. Rest 5 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. Start pedaling from stationary or slow pace
2. On "GO," sprint maximally for **30 seconds**
3. Resistance applied immediately
4. Pedal as fast as possible throughout
5. Strong verbal encouragement
6. Record power output every 5 seconds

**Measurements:**
- **Peak Power (W)** - highest 5-second output (usually first 5 sec)
- **Mean Power (W)** - average over 30 seconds
- **Minimum Power (W)** - lowest 5-second output
- **Fatigue Index (%)** = [(Peak - Minimum) / Peak] × 100

**Normative Data (W/kg):**

**Peak Power:**
- Elite Males: >12 W/kg
- Good Males: 10-12 W/kg
- Elite Females: >9 W/kg
- Good Females: 7-9 W/kg

**Mean Power:**
- Elite Males: >9 W/kg
- Good Males: 7-9 W/kg
- Elite Females: >7 W/kg
- Good Females: 5-7 W/kg

**Fatigue Index:**
- Low Fatigue: <40%
- Moderate: 40-50%
- High Fatigue: >50%

**Use:**
- Anaerobic capacity assessment
- Training monitoring
- Sport-specific for cycling, sprint sports

### 2.21 DYNAMIC STRENGTH INDEX (DSI)

**Purpose:** Compare ballistic strength to maximal strength  
**Equipment:** Force plate  
**Time:** 20 minutes  
**Calculation:** Ratio of CMJ to IMTP forces

**Protocol:**
1. Perform IMTP (see section 2.5)
   - Record Peak Force
2. Perform CMJ on force plate
   - Record Peak Force
3. Calculate DSI

**DSI Calculation:**
- **DSI = CMJ Peak Force / IMTP Peak Force**

**Example:**
- CMJ Peak Force: 2400N
- IMTP Peak Force: 3000N
- DSI = 2400 / 3000 = **0.80**

**Interpretation:**

| DSI | Profile | Training Implication |
|---|---|---|
| >0.80 | Strength-deficit | Focus on maximal strength (squats, deadlifts) |
| 0.60-0.80 | Balanced | Maintain both, sport-specific work |
| <0.60 | Ballistic-deficit | Focus on power/speed (plyometrics, Olympic lifts) |

**Use:**
- Identify training needs
- Monitor strength-power balance
- Individualize programming

**Normative:**
- **Sprinters/Jumpers:** 0.65-0.75 (more ballistic)
- **Strength Athletes:** 0.50-0.65 (more strength-oriented)
- **Team Sports:** 0.60-0.75 (balanced)

### 3.5 FLYING 10-METER SPRINT (Maximum Velocity)

### 3.11 CHANGE OF DIRECTION DEFICIT (CODD)

**Purpose:** Isolate COD ability from linear speed  
**Equipment:** Timing gates (505 + 10m sprint)  
**Time:** 20 minutes combined  
**Population:** Team sport athletes

**Protocol:**
1. Perform **505 Agility Test** (see 3.6)
   - Record best time
2. Perform **10m Sprint** (see 3.1)
   - Record best time
3. Calculate CODD

**CODD Calculation:**
- **CODD = 505 Time - 10m Sprint Time**

**Example:**
- 505 Time: 2.35 seconds
- 10m Sprint: 1.78 seconds
- CODD = 2.35 - 1.78 = **0.57 seconds**

**Interpretation:**

| CODD | Profile | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| <0.40s | Good COD technique | Efficient turning mechanics |
| 0.40-0.60s | Average | Balanced speed and COD |
| >0.60s | Poor COD | Needs agility/COD technical work |

**Use:**
- Differentiates fast runners who can't turn vs slower runners with good COD
- Identifies COD-specific deficits independent of linear speed
- Guides training focus: linear speed vs COD technique

**Training Implications:**
- **High CODD (>0.60s):** Focus on COD drills, deceleration, cutting technique
- **Low CODD (<0.40s):** COD is strength, maintain with skills work
- **Poor linear speed + High CODD:** Work on both qualities

## 5. AEROBIC CAPACITY

### 5.1 VO₂MAX TEST - BRUCE PROTOCOL (Treadmill)

**Purpose:** Measure maximal aerobic capacity  
**Equipment:** Treadmill, metabolic cart (gold standard) or HR monitor  
**Time:** 12-18 minutes  
**Population:** General population, endurance athletes

**Setup:**
1. Metabolic cart calibrated
2. HR monitor on athlete
3. Emergency stop accessible
4. Medical personnel present (if high-risk population)

**Bruce Protocol - 7 Stages (3 minutes each):**

| Stage | Speed (mph) | Speed (km/h) | Grade (%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1.7 | 2.7 | 10 |
| 2 | 2.5 | 4.0 | 12 |
| 3 | 3.4 | 5.5 | 14 |
| 4 | 4.2 | 6.8 | 16 |
| 5 | 5.0 | 8.0 | 18 |
| 6 | 5.5 | 8.9 | 20 |
| 7 | 6.0 | 9.7 | 22 |

**Procedure:**
1. Warm-up: 5 minutes light walking
2. Start Stage 1
3. Progress through stages
4. Continue until volitional exhaustion or termination criteria
5. Cool down: 5-10 minutes light walking

**Termination Criteria:**
- Volitional exhaustion (can't continue)
- Plateau in VO₂ despite increased workload
- RER >1.10
- HR within 10 bpm of age-predicted max
- ECG abnormalities (if monitored)
- Blood pressure concerns
- Athlete requests stop

**Measurements:**
- **VO₂max (ml/kg/min)** - Direct measurement from metabolic cart
- **Maximal Heart Rate (bpm)**
- **Respiratory Exchange Ratio (RER)** at max
- **Time to exhaustion**
- **Peak treadmill stage reached**

**Normative VO₂max (ml/kg/min):**

**Males:**
- Elite Endurance: >70
- Excellent: 55-70
- Good: 45-55
- Average: 35-45
- Below: <35

**Females:**
- Elite Endurance: >60
- Excellent: 45-60
- Good: 38-45
- Average: 28-38
- Below: <28

**Estimation (if no metabolic cart):**
- Various equations exist based on stage/time reached
- Less accurate than direct measurement

**Use:**
- Gold standard aerobic capacity
- Clinical populations
- Endurance athlete profiling

### 5.3 20-METER SHUTTLE RUN TEST (Beep Test / Léger Test)

**Purpose:** Estimate VO₂max via field test  
**Equipment:** Audio file (beeps), 20m marked distance, cones  
**Time:** 8-20 minutes depending on fitness  
**Population:** All athletes, especially team sports

**Setup:**
1. Mark two lines 20 meters apart
2. Play Beep Test audio (progressive speed)
3. Group of athletes can test simultaneously

**Procedure:**
1. Start at first beep
2. Run 20m to opposite line
3. Arrive before next beep
4. Turn and run back
5. Beeps get progressively faster
6. Continue until unable to keep pace
7. Record final level and shuttle completed

**Levels:**
- Start at Level 1 (slow)
- Each level = ~1 minute
- Speed increases each level
- Level 1: 8.5 km/h → Level 21: 18.0 km/h

**Termination:**
- Fails to reach line within beep tolerance (2m) twice

**Scoring:**
- Record: **Level.Shuttle** (e.g., 12.7 = Level 12, Shuttle 7)

**VO₂max Estimation (Léger Equation):**
- **VO₂max = 31.025 + 3.238×V - 3.248×A + 0.1536×V×A**
- V = final speed (km/h) at last completed stage
- A = age (years)

**Normative Scores:**

**Males (Level.Shuttle):**
- Excellent: >13.0 (~56+ ml/kg/min)
- Good: 11.0-13.0 (~48-56)
- Average: 9.0-11.0 (~42-48)
- Below: <9.0 (<42)

**Females (Level.Shuttle):**
- Excellent: >11.0 (~48+ ml/kg/min)
- Good: 9.5-11.0 (~40-48)
- Average: 7.5-9.5 (~35-40)
- Below: <7.5 (<35)

**Sport-Specific:**
- **Soccer/Rugby:** 12.0-14.0
- **Basketball:** 10.0-12.0
- **Tennis:** 9.0-11.0

**Use:**
- Simple, minimal equipment
- Group testing
- Standard in team sports worldwide

---

### 5.6 COOPER 12-MINUTE RUN TEST

**Purpose:** Estimate VO₂max via distance run in 12 minutes

### 5.9 30-15 INTERMITTENT FITNESS TEST (30-15 IFT)

**Purpose:** Determine Maximal Aerobic Speed (MAS) for training prescription  
**Equipment:** Audio file, 40m shuttle, cones  
**Time:** 10-20 minutes  
**Population:** Team sport athletes  
**Developer:** Martin Buchheit

**Setup:**
1. Mark 40-meter shuttle (20m each direction)
2. Audio provides pace
3. Start/finish line in middle (athlete shuttles 20m each way)

**Procedure:**
1. **30 seconds running** at set pace (shuttling 20m back and forth)
2. **15 seconds passive recovery** (stand/walk)
3. Speed increases each stage (0.5 km/h increments)
4. Starts at 8 km/h
5. Continue until unable to maintain pace
6. Record final stage completed

**Termination:**
- Fails to reach line on time twice
- Or cannot complete 40m in 30 seconds

**Scoring:**
- **VIFT (Velocity at Final Stage)** in km/h = Maximal Aerobic Speed (MAS)
- Used for training prescription

**Normative VIFT (km/h):**

**Team Sport Athletes:**
- Elite: 19.0-21.0 km/h
- Good: 17.0-19.0 km/h
- Average: 15.0-17.0 km/h

**VO₂max Estimation:**
- VO₂max = 28.3 - (2.15 × 1) - (0.741 × 40) - (0.0357 × 40) + (0.0586 × 1 × VIFT) + (1.03 × VIFT)

**Training Prescription:**
- Use VIFT to set interval training speeds
- Example: 120% VIFT for short intervals, 90% VIFT for tempo

**Advantage:**
- Directly gives MAS for programming
- Intermittent nature matches team sports
- More sport-specific than Beep Test

**Use:**
- European soccer standard
- Training intensity prescription
- Monitoring aerobic development

**Time:** 90 minutes  
**Population:** Soccer players  
**Nature:** Protocol, not assessment

**Overview:**
- **Not a test with "score"** - it's a standardized protocol
- Simulates soccer match: walking, jogging, cruising, sprinting
- 15-minute blocks × 6 = 90 minutes
- Each block has set pattern of speeds
- Used for research, match simulation training

**Speeds:**
- Walking: 1.5 m/s
- Jogging: 2.1 m/s (55% VO₂max)
- Cruising: 95% VO₂max
- Sprinting: Maximal

**Use:**
- Research protocol
- Pre-load for performance testing
- Match simulation
- Training tool

### 6.2 RUNNING-BASED ANAEROBIC SPRINT TEST (RAST)

**Purpose:** Running-specific anaerobic power  
**Equipment:** Track (6 × 35m marked), timing gates or stopwatch, cones  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Population:** Sprint and team sport athletes

**Setup:**
1. Mark 35-meter sprint distance
2. Timing gates at start/finish (or use stopwatch)
3. Start cone 1-2m before timing gates

**Procedure:**
1. Warm-up thoroughly
2. Perform **6 maximal 35m sprints**
3. **10 seconds rest** between sprints (walk back)
4. Record time for each sprint
5. Verbal encouragement on all sprints

**Measurements:**
- Time for each of 6 sprints (seconds)
- Calculate power for each sprint
- Calculate indices

**Power Calculation (Watts):**
- **Power = Body Mass (kg) × 35m² / Time³**

**Example:**
- Athlete: 80kg
- Sprint 1 time: 5.00 seconds
- Power = 80 × 35² / 5³ = 80 × 1225 / 125 = **784 Watts**

**Indices Calculated:**

1. **Peak Power (W)** - Highest power output (usually sprint 1)
2. **Mean Power (W)** - Average of all 6 sprints
3. **Minimum Power (W)** - Lowest power output (usually sprint 6)
4. **Fatigue Index (%)** = [(Peak - Minimum) / Peak] × 100

**Normative Data:**

**Peak Power (W/kg):**
- Elite: >12 W/kg
- Good: 10-12 W/kg
- Average: 8-10 W/kg
- Below: <8 W/kg

**Fatigue Index:**
- Low Fatigue: <30%
- Moderate: 30-40%
- High Fatigue: >40%

**Interpretation:**
- High peak, low fatigue: Well-developed anaerobic system
- High peak, high fatigue: Good power but poor repeat sprint ability
- Low peak, low fatigue: Limited power but good endurance
- Low peak, high fatigue: Needs development in both areas

**Use:**
- Running-specific alternative to Wingate
- Team sport assessment
- Repeat sprint ability profile

---

### 6.3 REPEAT SPRINT ABILITY TEST (6 × 20m)

**Purpose:** Assess repeat sprint ability and fatigue resistance  
**Equipment:** Timing gates, 20m course  
**Time:** 5 minutes  
**Rest:** **20 seconds** between sprints

**Setup:**
- 20-meter sprint course
- Timing gates at 0m and 20m

**Procedure:**
1. Perform **6 maximal 20m sprints**
2. **20 seconds rest** between sprints (active recovery - walk back)
3. Record time for each sprint

**Measurements:**
- Time for each sprint
- **Best Time (BT)** - Fastest sprint
- **Total Time (TT)** - Sum of all 6 sprints
- **Sprint Decrement (S-Dec%)** - Performance drop-off

**Sprint Decrement Calculation:**
- **S-Dec% = [(TT / (BT × 6)) - 1] × 100**

**Example:**
- Best Time: 3.00s
- Total Time: 18.60s
- S-Dec% = [(18.60 / 18.00) - 1] × 100 = **3.33%**

**Normative S-Dec%:**
- Excellent: <5%
- Good: 5-8%
- Average: 8-12%
- Poor: >12%

**Use:**
- Team sport specific
- Fatigue resistance
- Training monitoring

### 6.5 300-YARD SHUTTLE RUN

**Purpose:** Anaerobic endurance, lactate tolerance  
**Equipment:** 25-yard marked distance (basketball court)  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Population:** Team sports

**Females:**

**Use:**
- Anaerobic conditioning

## 7. ANTHROPOMETRY / BODY COMPOSITION

### 7.1 HEIGHT MEASUREMENT

**Purpose:** Basic anthropometric data  
**Equipment:** Stadiometer  
**Time:** 2 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. Stand barefoot, heels together
2. Heels, glutes, shoulders, head against wall
3. Look straight ahead (Frankfort plane)
4. Take deep breath, hold
5. Lower measuring arm to crown of head
6. Record to nearest 0.1 cm

---

### 7.2 BODY MASS (Weight)

**Purpose:** Track body mass  
**Equipment:** Digital scale  
**Time:** 2 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. Minimal clothing
2. Bladder empty
3. Consistent time of day (morning preferred)
4. Record to nearest 0.1 kg

---

### 7.3 BODY MASS INDEX (BMI)

**Purpose:** Screening tool for weight classification  
**Calculation:** BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)²

**Example:**
- Weight: 80kg, Height: 1.80m
- BMI = 80 / (1.80)² = **24.7**

**Classification:**
- Underweight: <18.5
- Normal: 18.5-24.9
- Overweight: 25.0-29.9
- Obese: ≥30.0

**Limitations:**
- Does not differentiate muscle vs fat
- Not appropriate for athletes
- Use body fat % instead

---

### 7.4 SKINFOLD MEASUREMENTS (3-Site)

**Purpose:** Estimate body fat percentage  
**Equipment:** Skinfold calipers (Lange, Harpenden)  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Tester:** Trained practitioner

**3-Site Protocol:**

**Males (Jackson & Pollock):**
1. **Chest:** Diagonal fold, midpoint between anterior axillary fold and nipple
2. **Abdomen:** Vertical fold, 2cm to right of umbilicus
3. **Thigh:** Vertical fold, midpoint between hip and knee (anterior thigh)

**Females (Jackson, Pollock & Ward):**
1. **Triceps:** Vertical fold, posterior midline of upper arm (halfway between acromion and olecranon)
2. **Suprailiac:** Diagonal fold, above iliac crest on mid-axillary line
3. **Thigh:** Same as males

**Procedure:**
1. Mark site precisely
2. Grasp skin and pull away from muscle
3. Apply calipers 1cm away from fingers
4. Wait 1-2 seconds, read measurement
5. Take 2-3 measurements per site, use median
6. Sum of 3 sites = Σ3

**Body Density Calculation:**

**Males:**
- BD = 1.10938 - (0.0008267 × Σ3) + (0.0000016 × Σ3²) - (0.0002574 × Age)

**Females:**
- BD = 1.0994921 - (0.0009929 × Σ3) + (0.0000023 × Σ3²) - (0.0001392 × Age)

**Body Fat % Calculation (Siri Equation):**
- **BF% = (495 / BD) - 450**

**Normative Body Fat %:**

**Males:**
- Essential: 2-5%
- Athletes: 6-13%
- Fitness: 14-17%
- Average: 18-24%
- Obese: ≥25%

**Females:**
- Essential: 10-13%
- Athletes: 14-20%
- Fitness: 21-24%
- Average: 25-31%
- Obese: ≥32%

**Sport-Specific:**
- Endurance runners: 5-10% (M), 10-16% (F)
- Sprinters: 6-12% (M), 12-18% (F)
- Team sports: 8-14% (M), 14-22% (F)

---

### 7.5 SKINFOLD MEASUREMENTS (7-Site)

**Purpose:** More comprehensive body fat estimate  
**Time:** 15 minutes

**7-Site Protocol (Jackson & Pollock):**
1. Chest
2. Abdomen
3. Thigh
4. Triceps
5. Subscapular (below shoulder blade)
6. Suprailiac
7. Midaxillary (below armpit)

**Body Density:**

**Males:**
- BD = 1.112 - (0.00043499 × Σ7) + (0.00000055 × Σ7²) - (0.00028826 × Age)

**Females:**
- BD = 1.097 - (0.00046971 × Σ7) + (0.00000056 × Σ7²) - (0.00012828 × Age)

**BF% = (495 / BD) - 450**

**Advantage:** More accurate than 3-site

### 7.7 WAIST-TO-HIP RATIO

**Purpose:** Central obesity and health risk assessment  
**Equipment:** Tape measure  
**Time:** 5 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. **Waist:** Narrowest point (usually above umbilicus) OR at umbilicus
2. **Hip:** Widest point over buttocks
3. Measure in cm
4. Calculate ratio

**Ratio = Waist / Hip**

**Health Risk Classification:**

**Males:**
- Low Risk: <0.95
- Moderate Risk: 0.95-1.0
- High Risk: >1.0

**Females:**
- Low Risk: <0.80
- Moderate Risk: 0.80-0.85
- High Risk: >0.85

**Use:**
- Health screening
- Central adiposity indicator
- Cardiovascular risk

---

### 7.8 GIRTH MEASUREMENTS (7 Sites)

**Purpose:** Track regional body composition changes  
**Equipment:** Tape measure (Gulick)  
**Time:** 15 minutes

**Sites:**
1. **Shoulders:** Widest point, arms at sides
2. **Chest:** Nipple line, normal breath
3. **Waist:** Narrowest point or umbilicus
4. **Hips:** Widest point over glutes
5. **Thigh:** Widest point (or 1 inch below gluteal fold)
6. **Calf:** Widest point
7. **Arm (Bicep):** Widest point, arm relaxed at side

**Procedure:**
- Tape snug but not compressing tissue
- Perpendicular to body segment
- Record to nearest 0.5 cm
- Take 2-3 measurements, use average

**Use:**
- Track muscle hypertrophy
- Monitor regional fat loss
- Supplement to skinfolds

---

### 7.9 BIOELECTRICAL IMPEDANCE ANALYSIS (BIA)

**Purpose:** Estimate body composition via electrical conductivity  
**Equipment:** BIA device (e.g., InBody, Tanita)  
**Time:** 10 minutes

**Procedure:**
1. Pre-test requirements:
   - No exercise 12 hours prior
   - No eating/drinking 4 hours prior
   - Bladder empty
   - No alcohol 24 hours prior
   - Hydration status controlled
2. Stand on device (foot-to-foot) OR hold handles (hand-to-hand) OR both
3. Electrical signal sent through body
4. Resistance measured
5. Body composition calculated via equations

**Measurements:**
- Body Fat %
- Lean Body Mass (kg)
- Total Body Water
- Muscle Mass
- Bone Mass (estimated)

**Limitations:**
- Hydration affects accuracy
- Equation-dependent
- Less accurate than DEXA or hydrostatic weighing
- More accurate than BMI

**Use:**
- Convenient, quick
- Track trends over time
- Consistent testing conditions critical

## 9. TESTING SESSION GUIDELINES

### 9.1 PRE-TEST REQUIREMENTS

**48 Hours Before:**
- No maximal training
- Adequate sleep
- No alcohol

**24 Hours Before:**
- No heavy resistance training
- No excessive eccentric exercise
- Adequate hydration
- Normal diet

**Day of Testing:**
- Light meal 2-3 hours prior (if applicable)
- Hydrated
- No caffeine (or standardized caffeine intake)
- Proper warm-up
- Rested

**Contraindications:**
- Illness
- Injury
- Extreme fatigue
- Recent concussion (for relevant tests)

---

### 9.2 RECOMMENDED TESTING ORDER (Multi-Day)

**Session 1: Anthropometry & Movement Screening**
- Height, Weight, Skinfolds
- FMS or Y-Balance
- Flexibility tests
- Movement assessments

**Session 2: Power & Speed**
- CMJ, Broad Jump
- Sprint tests (10m, 20m, 30m)
- RSI / Drop Jumps
- (Morning preferred - neural freshness)

**Session 3: Strength**
- IMTP
- 1RM testing (if applicable)
- (Separate day or afternoon after power)

**Session 4: Agility & COD**
- 505, Illinois, T-Test
- Sport-specific agility

**Session 5: Endurance**
- Beep Test, Yo-Yo, or other aerobic test
- (Separate day, can be AM or PM)

**Special Tests (as needed):**
- NordBord, GroinBar: Session 1 or 2
- Wingate/RAST: Session 5 or separate

---

### 9.3 SAME-DAY TESTING ORDER

**If all tests must be done in one day:**

1. **Anthropometry** (Height, Weight, Skinfolds)
2. **Movement Screens** (FMS, Y-Balance)
3. **Flexibility** (Sit and Reach, etc.)
4. **Agility** (T-Test, 505) - submaximal warm-up
5. **Power** (CMJ, Broad Jump)
6. **Speed** (10m, 20m, 30m)
7. **Strength** (IMTP, 1RM if needed)
8. **Endurance** (Beep Test) - LAST

**Rationale:** Neural → Strength → Endurance (fatigue progression)

---

### 9.4 WARM-UP PROTOCOLS

**General Warm-Up (All Tests):**
1. 5-10 minutes light aerobic (jogging, cycling)
2. Dynamic stretching (10 minutes)
3. Movement prep specific to test

**Power/Speed Tests:**
1. General warm-up
2. Progressive build-ups
3. Submaximal jumps or sprints
4. Rest 3-5 minutes before testing

**Strength Tests:**
1. General warm-up
2. Specific warm-up sets (progressive loading)
3. Adequate rest between warm-up and testing

**Endurance Tests:**
1. Light 5-10 minute warm-up
2. Some dynamic stretching
3. No fatiguing efforts

---

### 9.5 ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS

**Temperature:**
- Ideal: 18-22°C
- Avoid extreme heat or cold

**Humidity:**
- Moderate preferred
- High humidity affects endurance performance

**Altitude:**
- Sea level normative data most common
- Adjust expectations at altitude

**Surface:**
- Consistent, non-slip
- Indoor preferred for standardization

**Time of Day:**
- Standardize for repeat testing
- Morning: Better for power/neural tests
- Afternoon: Slightly better for strength/endurance
- Avoid early AM (circadian trough)

---

### 9.6 SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

**General:**
- Medical screening (PAR-Q or equivalent)
- Trained testers
- Emergency action plan
- First aid available

**Maximal Strength Testing:**
- Spotters mandatory
- Proper technique only
- Stop if form breaks down

**Maximal Aerobic Testing:**
- Medical clearance for high-risk individuals
- Emergency equipment available
- Termination criteria clear

**Injury History:**
- Modify tests based on injury
- Return-to-play protocols
- Medical clearance

**Baseline/Pre-Season:**

- Progressive testing per protocol
- Weekly until cleared
