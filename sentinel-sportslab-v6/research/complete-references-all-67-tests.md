  - **FREE ACCESS:** https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3419550/

**Long-Lever vs Short-Lever:**

- **Light, N., & Thorborg, K. (2016).** The precision and torque production of common hip adductor squeeze tests used in elite football. *Journal of Science and Medicine in Sport*, 19(11), 888-892.

- **Nielsen, M.F., Thorborg, K., Krommes, K., Gudbergsen, H., Hölmich, P., & Ishøi, L. (2022).** Hip adduction strength and provoked groin pain: A comparison of long-lever squeeze testing using the ForceFrame and the Copenhagen 5-Second-Squeeze test. *Physical Therapy in Sport*, 55, 28-36.
  - DOI: 10.1016/j.ptsp.2022.01.005
  - Long-lever: 15% higher torque, more pain-sensitive

**Abduction Tests:**

- **Ishøi, L., Thorborg, K., Krohn, L., Andersen, L.L., Nielsen, A.M., & Clausen, M.B. (2023).** Maximal and explosive muscle strength during hip adduction squeeze and hip abduction press test using a handheld dynamometer: An intra- and inter-tester reliability study. *International Journal of Sports Physical Therapy*, 18(4), 905-916.
  - **FREE ACCESS:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10399103/
  - Both adduction AND abduction reliability
  - Long-lever protocol

**GroinBar/ForceFrame Specific:**

- **Desmyttere, G., Gaudet, S., & Begon, M. (2019).** Test-retest reliability of a hip strength assessment system in varsity soccer players. *Physical Therapy in Sport*, 37, 138-143.
  - DOI: 10.1016/j.ptsp.2019.03.013
  - GroinBar reliability (ICC 0.77-0.95)

- **O'Brien, J., Bourne, M.N., Heerey, J., Timmins, R.G., & Pizzari, T. (2019).** A novel device to assess hip strength: a reliability and validity study. *Journal of Science and Medicine in Sport*, 22(4), 406-411.
  - DOI: 10.1016/j.jsams.2018.09.233
  - ForceFrame validation in Australian Football

**Add:Abd Ratio & Injury:**

- **Moreno-Pérez, V., Travassos, B., Calado, A., Gonzalo-Skok, O., Del Coso, J., & Mendez-Villanueva, A. (2019).** Adductor squeeze test and groin injuries in elite football players: A prospective study. *Physical Therapy in Sport*, 37, 54-59.
