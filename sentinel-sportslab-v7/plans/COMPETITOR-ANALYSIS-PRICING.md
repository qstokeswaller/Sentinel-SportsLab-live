# Competitor Analysis & Pricing Models

## Overview

This document compares sport science and athlete monitoring platforms by pricing model, feature depth, and market positioning. The goal is to inform Sentinel SportsLab's pricing strategy.

**Research date:** April 2026
**Note:** Some platforms are enterprise-only with no public pricing. Where exact numbers aren't available, ranges are based on industry knowledge and reported figures. Actual pricing may have changed.

## Suggested Pricing Model for Sentinel SportsLab

Based on competitor analysis, the market supports these tiers:

### Option A: Per-Athlete Model (most common in this space)

| Tier | Price | Includes | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Free / Trial** | $0 (14 days) | Full access, 5 athletes | Try before you buy |
| **Starter** | $3/athlete/mo | Core features: roster, workouts, exercise library, basic testing, scheduling | Personal trainers, small gyms |
| **Pro** | $6/athlete/mo | Everything in Starter + ACWR, wellness hub, GPS import, smart CSV, analytics, injury tracking | Sport scientists, S&C coaches, small clubs |
| **Elite** | $10/athlete/mo | Everything in Pro + individualized thresholds, F-V profiling, dose-response, scenario modelling, Performance Intelligence, benchmarking | Pro sport, national federations, universities |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | White-label, API access, SSO, dedicated support, data migration | Large organizations |

**Annual discount:** 20% off (effectively 2 months free)
**Minimum:** None for Starter, 10 athletes for Pro/Elite

### Option B: Flat Monthly (simpler, predictable)

| Tier | Price | Athletes | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Solo** | $29/mo | Up to 15 athletes | Personal trainers, private clients |
| **Team** | $79/mo | Up to 50 athletes | Single team, small club |
| **Club** | $149/mo | Up to 150 athletes | Multi-team organizations |
| **Unlimited** | $299/mo | Unlimited | Large organizations, universities |

### Recommendation

**Option A (per-athlete)** is industry standard and scales naturally. It also incentivises feature adoption — coaches upgrade when they need deeper analytics, not just more athletes.

The **Pro tier at $6/athlete/month** is the sweet spot:
- 2x cheaper than CoachMePlus advanced tier
- 100x cheaper than Smartabase (at team scale)
- Significantly more sport science depth than TeamBuildr/TrainHeroic at a similar price
- Self-service onboarding means zero sales cost

## Detailed Competitor Feature Inventory

### Self-Service Tier (Direct Competitors)

**Athlete Monitoring** (wellness questionnaires, ACWR monitoring, injury tracking, reporting dashboards, API access, custom branding on higher tiers)
- We have all of this and more. Nothing to add from them.

**TeamBuildr** (program design, exercise video library, testing/assessments, team management, **athlete-facing mobile app with workout delivery**, **rep max calculators built into workout logging**)
- The **athlete-facing app** is worth noting — athletes log workouts on their phone with built-in timers and calculators. We don't have a dedicated athlete app experience yet.

**TrainHeroic** (program design, workout logging, exercise videos, team management, **marketplace for selling programs**, **leaderboards/social features**, **community/follower system**)
- The **marketplace model** is interesting — coaches sell programs to the public and TrainHeroic takes a cut. Creates network effects and passive revenue for coaches.
- **Leaderboards** — athletes compete on metrics (1RM, workout completion). We don't have gamification.

**Bridge Athletic** (program design, exercise library, **movement assessment flows with guided video**, **athlete mobile app with push notifications**, progress tracking)
- **Guided assessment flows** with video instructions for each movement screen — polished athlete-side UX.

**CoachMePlus** (workout programming, wellness monitoring, readiness scores, **force plate integrations via API**, reporting, **white-labeling on higher tiers**, **athlete mobile app**, **kiosk mode for team check-ins**)
- **Kiosk mode** — iPad at the training ground where athletes tap in and do their wellness check. We don't have this.
- **White-labeling** — coaches brand the platform as their own. We don't offer this yet.

### Sales-Led Tier

**RockDaisy** (data aggregation from multiple sources, **unified data warehouse**, visualisation dashboards, **API-first architecture for pulling data from GPS/force plates/wearables into one place**)
- Their strength is being a **data aggregation layer** — they don't collect data themselves, they pull from Catapult, VALD, Polar etc. into one view. Our third-party API plan covers this eventually.

**Output Sports** (**hardware IMU sensor**, VBT tracking, jump profiling, F-V profiling, **normative database with thousands of athletes for percentile comparison**, exercise library)
- Their **normative database** is massive — when an athlete jumps, they instantly see percentile vs thousands of athletes by sport/position/age. Our benchmarking is roster-level only currently.

### Enterprise Tier

**Smartabase / Fusion Sport** (**extreme form customisation**, workflow automation, **role-based access control** — physio sees different data than coach, **compliance/audit trails**, medical modules, **data integration hub with 50+ partners**, **multi-language support**)
- **Role-based access** is significant — a physio sees medical records, an S&C coach sees training data, a head coach sees availability only. We show everything to everyone currently.
- **Compliance/audit trails** — who changed what, when. Important for medical-legal contexts.

**Kitman Labs** (**AI injury prediction models**, workload optimisation, **automated insight generation with natural language explanations**, medical record integration, **insurance/claims integration**)
- Their **AI injury prediction** is their moat — proprietary models trained on years of pro sport data. We have rule-based risk flags but not predictive ML.

**Catapult AMS / Kinduct** (athlete management, wellness, **seamless real-time GPS data feed from Catapult hardware**, reporting, **session planning with GPS load targets**, **live session monitoring dashboards**)
- **Live session monitoring** — during training, see real-time GPS metrics per player on a dashboard. Hardware-dependent.

## Features to Consider Adding (Gap Analysis)

Based on what competitors offer that we currently don't:

### High Priority (differentiators that affect pricing tier justification)

1. **Athlete-facing mobile app / workout delivery** (TeamBuildr, CoachMePlus, Bridge)
   - Athletes do their workouts on their phone with timers, rest counters, logging
   - Our workouts are coach-side only currently
   - Impact: Justifies higher pricing, increases daily engagement

2. **Role-based access control** (Smartabase)
   - Different views for physio vs S&C vs head coach
   - As we add sensitive medical data (injury classifications, RTP protocols), this becomes important
   - Impact: Required for enterprise tier, medical-legal compliance

3. **Kiosk mode for team check-ins** (CoachMePlus)
   - iPad at the training ground door, athletes tap their name and do daily wellness
   - Fast, frictionless, no links to send
   - Impact: Dramatically improves daily compliance rates

### Medium Priority (growth features)

4. **White-labeling** (CoachMePlus, Smartabase)
   - Coaches brand the platform as their own
   - Premium feature that justifies enterprise pricing
   - Impact: Revenue tier unlock

5. **Marketplace for selling programs** (TrainHeroic)
   - Coaches create and sell training programs publicly
   - Creates passive income and platform stickiness
   - Impact: Network effects, different business model for Starter/Solo tier

6. **Normative database expansion** (Output Sports)
   - Large-scale percentile comparison beyond just the coach's own roster
   - Sport/position/age-specific norms from aggregated platform data
   - Impact: Grows more valuable as more users join the platform

### Lower Priority (nice-to-have)

7. **Gamification / leaderboards** (TrainHeroic)
   - Athletes compete on metrics within their team
   - More relevant for gen pop / CrossFit than pro sport
   - Impact: Engagement for certain market segments

8. **AI injury prediction** (Kitman Labs)
   - ML models trained on large datasets to predict injury probability
   - Requires significant data accumulation first
   - Our rule-based flags + individualized thresholds are the stepping stone toward this

9. **Live session monitoring** (Catapult AMS)
   - Real-time dashboards during training sessions
   - Requires third-party API integration (GPS hardware dependent)
   - Impact: Pro sport feature, builds on our GPS integration plan

10. **Multi-language support** (Smartabase)
    - Important for international market expansion
    - Impact: Market reach

### What we already have that others don't
- Individualized load tolerance thresholds (nobody)
- Force-velocity profiling from existing tests without hardware (nobody)
- Dose-response analysis per athlete per block (nobody cleanly)
- Smart CSV auto-detection across all test types (nobody)
- Scenario modelling for ACWR planning (nobody)
- FIFA/IOC-aligned wellness form system (planned, nobody has this as a product feature)
- Wattbike MAP calculator (nobody in this category)
- Conditioning hub with running mechanics (nobody in this category)

## Smaller / Lesser-Known Competitors

### UK / Europe

**Edge10 Group** — UK-based AMS used by Premier League and Championship football clubs. Covers wellness, injury tracking, load monitoring, and testing. Enterprise/bespoke pricing. Unique: deep integration with UK football medical workflows and club administrative systems.

**The Tipping Point** — UK-based load monitoring and decision-support platform. Used by some rugby and football clubs. Focuses on injury risk reduction through workload analysis. Bespoke pricing.

**Orreco** — Irish biomarker analytics company. Blood biomarker testing with AI-driven recommendations for recovery, training load, and female athlete health. Enterprise pricing. Unique: one of very few platforms doing blood-based monitoring at scale — a completely different approach to readiness (biological, not perceptual).

**Firstbeat Sports (now Garmin Sports)** — Finland, HRV-based training load and recovery monitoring. Was standalone, now integrated into Garmin ecosystem. Still available as a team solution. ~$1,000-3,000/year per team. Unique: strong HRV analytics heritage, well-validated physiology models.

### Australia

**Performa Sports** — Video analysis + performance data tools from Australia. Used in AFL, rugby, cricket. Bespoke pricing. Not a pure AMS but has performance tracking capabilities.

**Sparta Science** — US-based but big in Australia and military. Force plate platform using ML to generate proprietary "Sparta Signatures" for injury risk. Enterprise pricing (hardware + subscription). Unique: proprietary algorithm, though the underlying science has been debated in literature.

### South Africa

**iP Sport** — One of very few SA-built sport science platforms. Used by some SA rugby franchises. Covers testing, monitoring, planning. Pricing unknown. **This is our most direct local competitor.**

Beyond iP Sport, the SA market is thin for homegrown platforms. Most SA rugby franchises (Bulls, Sharks, Stormers) and Cricket South Africa use Smartabase or Catapult AMS. Some university sport science departments (Stellenbosch, UCT, NWU) have built internal tools but none have been commercialised. **This is a genuine gap in the market — and directly relevant to our positioning.**

### Simpler / Lightweight Platforms

**Hurdle** — Athlete wellness and check-in platform. Deliberately lightweight, targets collegiate and club-level teams. ~$5-15/athlete/month. Unique: simplicity is the product — quick daily wellness surveys with minimal overhead. Competes on ease-of-use, not depth.

**Fytt Health** — Athlete wellness and readiness monitoring. Small footprint, targets pro and semi-pro teams. Pricing unclear. May no longer be actively marketed as standalone.

### Hardware-Tied Platforms (free with device purchase)

**VALD Hub** — VALD's central cloud platform connecting ForceDecks, NordBord, DynaMo, AirBands, HumanTrak, SmartSpeed, GymAware. Free with hardware. Unique: the ecosystem play — one dashboard for all their testing modalities. **Increasingly acting as a mini-AMS. Worth watching — if they expand into wellness/load monitoring, they become a serious competitor.** Currently testing-only.

**Hawkin Dynamics Cloud** — Cloud platform tied to their wireless force plates. Covers jump testing, isometric testing, DSI, RSI. Included with hardware purchase. Unique: very clean dashboard, automatic asymmetry and fatigue metrics from force-time data.

**GymAware Cloud (now under VALD)** — VBT and power profiling platform tied to GymAware/FLEX hardware. Included with device. Extensive load-velocity profiling norms.

**Metric VBT** — Affordable VBT app ($10-20/month). Uses phone camera or compatible sensors for velocity tracking. Niche but well-regarded in VBT coaching circles. Targets individual S&C coaches who can't afford GymAware hardware.

### Rehab / Medical Adjacent

**Physitrack / PhysiApp** — Rehab exercise prescription and patient monitoring. Not sport-specific but widely used by sports physiotherapists. ~$30-80/month per practitioner. Massive exercise video library, patient compliance tracking. Used alongside AMS platforms, not as a replacement.

**Presagia Sports** — Canadian injury and illness tracking platform. Strong in MLS and some NCAA programs. Focus is medical records, return-to-play protocols, regulatory compliance. Enterprise pricing.

### Newer / Niche

**JOGO** — Football-specific wearable and app from India. Sensor-based skill tracking (kicking, dribbling). Consumer/academy pricing. Targets football skill development rather than physical load monitoring.

**Recovery Science** — Neuromuscular fatigue monitoring using assessment protocols. Small company targeting applied sport science practitioners. Pricing unclear.

**MacroFactor** — Nutrition tracking with adaptive TDEE algorithm. $6-12/month. Not AMS but increasingly used alongside monitoring platforms by performance nutritionists. Worth noting for potential nutrition module integration.

## Strategic Observations

### The competitive landscape in layers

```
Enterprise ($10K-100K+/yr)
├── Smartabase, Kitman Labs, Catapult AMS, Edge10
├── Target: Pro sport, Olympic programs, military
├── Sales cycles: 3-12 months
└── We don't compete here (yet)

Mid-Market ($3-10/athlete/mo)
├── CoachMePlus, Athlete Monitoring, Hurdle
├── Target: College S&C, semi-pro, large clubs
├── *** THIS IS WHERE WE SIT ***
└── We win on depth + self-service

Accessible ($1-4/athlete/mo)
├── TeamBuildr, TrainHeroic, Bridge Athletic
├── Target: PTs, gyms, high school, gen pop
├── We compete here on our Starter tier
└── They win on simplicity, we win on depth

Hardware-Bundled (free with device)
├── VALD Hub, Hawkin Cloud, GymAware Cloud
├── Target: Labs, testing centres
├── Not direct competitors but worth monitoring
└── VALD Hub expansion is the main threat

Niche Single-Purpose
├── Metric VBT, Physitrack, Orreco, Sparta Science
├── Not full AMS competitors
└── Potential integration partners rather than competitors
```

### Key threats to watch
1. **VALD Hub expanding into wellness/load monitoring** — they already have the testing hardware moat and a growing cloud platform
2. **TrainHeroic adding sport science features** — they have the largest coach user base and marketplace network effects
3. **Garmin/Firstbeat pushing a team monitoring platform** — they own the wearable data pipeline

### Our defensible advantages
1. **No hardware dependency** — works with any device's CSV/API data
2. **Self-service at enterprise depth** — no demo call needed for features that cost $10K+ elsewhere
3. **FIFA/IOC-aligned wellness system** — nobody else has this as a product feature
4. **South African market position** — virtually no local competition
5. **Individualized analytics** — personal load thresholds, dose-response, F-V profiling from existing tests
