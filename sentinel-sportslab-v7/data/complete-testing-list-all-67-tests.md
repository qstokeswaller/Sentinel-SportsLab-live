## **RETURN-TO-PLAY TESTING BATTERIES:**

### **ACL Reconstruction:**
- Hop Testing Battery (all 4 tests)
- CMJ with Asymmetry Analysis
- Isokinetic Knee Strength (Quads/Hamstrings)
- Y-Balance Test
- Agility Tests (505, T-Test)
- **Criteria:** >90% Limb Symmetry Index

### **Hamstring Strain:**
- NordBord Eccentric Strength
- IMTP
- Sprint Testing (10m, Flying 10m)
- Single Leg Bridge Test
- **Criteria:** >340N NordBord, <10% asymmetry

### **Groin Injury:**
- GroinBar Adduction/Abduction
- Copenhagen 5-Second Squeeze
- Adduction:Abduction Ratio
- **Criteria:** Add:Abd >0.80, Pain <2/10

### **Ankle Sprain:**
- Y-Balance Test (Anterior reach deficit <4cm)
- Single Leg Balance
- Hop Testing
- Ankle Dorsiflexion ROM
- **Criteria:** >90% LSI, balance symmetry

## **TESTING FREQUENCY RECOMMENDATIONS:**

### **Pre-Season / Baseline:**
- **Comprehensive Battery:** All relevant tests for sport
- **Timing:** 4-6 weeks before competition starts
- **Purpose:** Establish baselines, identify deficits

### **In-Season Monitoring:**
- **Weekly/Bi-Weekly:**
  - CMJ (RSImod for monitoring)
  - Wellness questionnaires
  - Optional: GroinBar, NordBord
  
- **Monthly:**
  - Sprint tests (10m, 20m)
  - Jump battery
  - Agility tests

- **Mid-Season (every 8-12 weeks):**
  - Strength tests (IMTP, or 3-5RM loads)
  - Endurance tests
  - Movement screens

### **Post-Season:**
- **End of Season Testing:**
  - Full comprehensive battery
  - Compare to pre-season baselines
  - Identify areas for off-season focus

### **Off-Season:**
- **Every 4-6 Weeks:**
  - Strength progressions (1RM or IMTP)
  - Power development (CMJ, Broad Jump)
  - Speed/Agility maintenance
  - Body composition monitoring

### **Return-to-Play:**
- **Weekly progression** until criteria met
- Sport-specific battery based on injury

## **AGE-APPROPRIATE TESTING:**

### **Youth Athletes (U12-U15):**
**Appropriate Tests:**
- FMS or Y-Balance (screening)
- CMJ, Broad Jump (relative power)
- 10m/20m Sprint
- Beep Test or Yo-Yo
- Basic flexibility tests
- Bodyweight movement assessments

**AVOID:**
- 1RM testing (use 3-5RM or bodyweight)
- High-intensity plyometrics (high drop heights)
- Excessive volume testing

### **Adolescents (U16-U18):**
**Add to youth battery:**
- NordBord (from U16+)
- GroinBar Adduction/Abduction
- IMTP (from U17+)
- 1RM or 3RM testing (with proper supervision)
- Drop Jump/RSI (moderate heights)

### **Adults (18+):**
**Full testing battery appropriate**
- All tests can be administered
- Adjust for training age and experience
- Consider injury history

### **Masters Athletes (40+):**
**Modify:**
- Emphasize movement quality over maximal output
- Reduce plyometric intensity
- Focus on mobility/flexibility assessments
- Monitor joint health
- Consider cardiovascular screening
